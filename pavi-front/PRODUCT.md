# Product

## Register

product

## Users

PAVI is used by internal administrative and logistics staff who need to access fleet operations quickly during the workday. The login screen is the controlled entry point for authenticated users before they reach trips, drivers, vehicles, shippers, dashboards, freight, and user management.

## Product Purpose

PAVI centralizes fleet management workflows for trips, vehicles, drivers, shippers, freight data, KPIs, and operational filtering. The product should help internal teams trust the data, find the right operational area fast, and continue work without visual friction. Success means the interface feels clear, secure, and purpose-built for the transport operation instead of looking like a generic SaaS shell.

## Brand Personality

Clean corporate, operationally reliable, and restrained. The interface should feel professional and precise, with enough transport identity to belong to PAVI without turning every screen into marketing.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic startup hero pages, decorative glass cards, oversized marketing headlines, abstract gradient blobs, stock-SaaS copy, and visuals that could apply to any logistics product. Avoid dark "command center" styling unless a specific operational context requires it.

## Design Principles

- Lead with task trust: authentication, navigation, and operational data should look dependable before they look expressive.
- Keep the brand present but quiet: PAVI identity should appear through logo, color discipline, transport-specific details, and copy, not decoration.
- Prefer product familiarity: standard controls, readable labels, clear focus states, and predictable responsive behavior matter more than novelty.
- Use density with control: internal users can handle compact UI when hierarchy and spacing remain clear.
- Make state visible: loading, errors, disabled actions, and authentication feedback should be explicit and accessible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text, controls, focus states, and error feedback. Motion should be minimal and respect reduced-motion preferences. The login must work on desktop and mobile, support keyboard-only operation, and keep error messages clear in Portuguese.
